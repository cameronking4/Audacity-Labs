About Us

What We Do

Audacity Labs is a start-up incubator program for teens, where teams of student entrepreneurs will ​work together to conceive, develop, and launch a start-up enterprise​. This will be done through an intense structured program of content and coaching, integrating a core set of skills: l​ ean start-up​, ​design thinking​, and c​ oding​. The program is the result of a collaboration of institutions with expertise across these facets of entrepreneurial learning, including: the M​ useum of Life and Science​, D​ uke University’s Innovation and Entrepreneurship initiative,​ ​American Underground​, and the ReCity Network.​

We want to empower local 9th-12th grade students with the tools and resources to effectively develop a start-up commercial or social enterprise. The program will enable participants to zoom in on a specific issue or customer need, prototype ideas to address that need,, develop a new product or service, and eventually lead to a sustained enterprise. In so doing, students will develop key 21st century skills associated with the start-up process, including problem-solving, creativity, confident communications, computational thinking, and collaboration.

Our Program

Start-Up Incubator

Students will have the opportunity to engage with local startup incubators and non-profit co-working spaces and be exposed to a variety of CEOs, Duke Faculty, VCs, founders, and undergraduate faculty across a wide range of industries


Student cohorts will be divided into teams of four to six individuals. Each team will focus on the development of a start-up, with either a commercial orientation, a social enterprise, or a hybrid of both.

Note: Students will be actively recruited from both traditional audiences and underserved populations, creating teams of students with a diversity of interests, strengths, and backgrounds. This recruitment will be enabled by pre-existing relationships that our collaborating institutions have with: Durham Public Schools, Chapel Hill Carrboro Public Schools, Citizen Schools, Durham Academy, and the Duke School.

Methodology

Structure & Support for Growth

A customized, structured program which integrates components of several existing methods and materials, notably Google Ventures’ Design Sprint methodology​ IDEO.org’s DesignKit​, and Code.Org’s App Lab​. The combination of our collaborating organizations includes extensive experience in all of these, and their use with learners of various ages.

Curriculum

How We Teach

The bulk of the program activity will be a series of workshops applying design thinking, and roughly following the Design Sprint method mentioned above. In camp form, each week of the program will form a single ‘Sprint’, or iteration, of the concept development. In this method, each day progresses through one step of the overall process of: research, ideation, design, prototyping, and testing. In year-long club form, a single sprint will be distributed over several weeks to accommodate students’ school-year demands.

Using open source coding curricula curated by our partner CSbyUs, participants will receive introductory training in coding principles, and those students who express interest in software/web/app development, will be provided with the necessary materials (e.g. hardware, software, and training) for more advanced training.

Mentorship

Mentor Driven Programming

Team Coaches:​ Student teams will have the support of full-time Coaches - university students and recent graduates - who themselves will receive training in facilitation and project-based learning. Coaches will be the core delivery agents for workshops and development activity, and support teams through the entire process.

Professional Mentoring:​ Drawn from the combined network of our collaborating partners, a selected set of professional mentors will provide targeted presentations and guidance to participants in a range of areas key to start-up development, including: leadership development, design, team building, financial modeling, communications. Mentors may be Duke faculty or alumni, members of American Underground, ReCity or the Museum of Life and Science, social entrepreneurs, venture capitalists, scientists, etc.
